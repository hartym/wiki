## Introduction

Quartal voicings are built by stacking intervals of 4ths rather than the traditional 3rds used in tertian harmony. The standard quartal voicing uses 5 notes, though this can be reduced to 3-4 notes or expanded to 6 notes.

* **Harmonic Ambiguity**: Quartal chords are ambiguous. There is no clear tonal center, and each voicing can represent many different chords.
* **Modern Sound**: Building chords in 4ths is considered sophisticated and «modern» compared to traditional tertian harmony.
* **Suspended Quality**: Due to their construction in 4ths, these voicings have a slightly suspended chord sound.

      <i>Note: same voicings are available for minor 7, the only difference is the 4th function, which is written as 4 here (because it replaces the third) while it's

    </td>
  </tr>
</table>

#### Major 7

* ♯4th → ♯4, 7, 3
* 7th → 7, 3, 6
* 3rd → 3, 6, 9
* 6th → 6, 9, 5
* 9th → 9, 5, Root

#### Minor 7♭5 (half-diminished)

* Root → R, 11,♭7
* 11th → 11, ♭7, ♭3
* ♭7th → ♭7,♭3,♭13
